About the July-August Movement

In the summer of 2024, hundreds of students and protesters in Bangladesh were subjected to violence and persecution by the regime of Sheikh Hasina. The anti-discrimination movement aimed to bring attention to widespread government oppression. However, this peaceful movement was met with brutality, resulting in the deaths of many young lives.

Why We Collect Data

This project, Equal Bangladesh, seeks to collect, document, and preserve the stories and facts surrounding the tragic events of July-August 2024. By gathering this data, we aim to give a voice to the victims and hold those responsible for atrocities accountable. The collected data will serve as a historical record of the events and help in future justice-seeking efforts.
